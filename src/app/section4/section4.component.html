How Disease Progression Affects the Body

As scarring progresses, you may begin to feel effects on your body, including persistent symptoms like:


  • shortness of breath

  • a dry, hacking cough

  • rapid, shallow breathing

  • fatigue

  • gradual weight loss

  • aching muscles and joints

  • widening and rounding of the fingertips or toes

Symptoms may worsen to the point that they take a toll on your well-being—from your emotional state to your ability to complete everyday tasks.

“I began having trouble doing simple things. I could shop for my groceries without any problem, but bringing them in the house left me huffing and puffing. I needed to rest before I could put my groceries away. Any type of exercise that required a little more effort caused me to lose my breath.”


— Gene, PERSON LIVING WITH IPF, A COMMON TYPE OF ILD

Pulmonologists will track the effect of chronic ILD with worsening fibrosis on the body with certain tests , like HRCT scans, lung function tests such as forced vital capacity (FVC), and walking tests.


While these tests can help provide a clearer picture of how chronic ILD may be worsening, remember that one single test may not tell the whole story. Even if one test result remains the same, chronic ILD with fibrosis may still be worsening.


Additionally, it’s important to understand that test results aren’t just numbers on a chart—they may help explain why you’re feeling a certain way. That’s why it’s important to talk to your healthcare provider when your symptoms change.


HELPFUL HINT

Tests used to detect chronic ILD with worsening fibrosis include:

  • HRCT scans
  • Pulse oximetry (measured at rest and with exertion)
  • Exercise/walking tests
  • Spirometry (forced vital capacity [FVC])
  • Diffusing capacity of the lung for carbon monoxide (DLCO)
  • Lung volume measurement (total lung capacity [TLC])
  • Arterial blood gas testing
  • Questionnaire, like the University of California San Diego Shortness of Breath Questionnaire
