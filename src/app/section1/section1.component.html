What Is ILD Progression?

ILD may cause scarring of the lungs (called pulmonary fibrosis) that gets worse, or progresses, over time. You may hear this referred to as having a chronic ILD with worsening fibrosis.


Progression in chronic ILD can present itself many different ways.


Some people may notice worsening symptoms, see changes in tests , like high-resolution computed tomography (HRCT) scans or lung function tests, or have to go on supplemental oxygen, while others may not notice any signs of progression for a long time.

HELPFUL HINT

Tests used to detect chronic ILD with worsening fibrosis include:

  • HRCT scans
  • Pulse oximetry (measured at rest and with exertion)
  • Exercise/walking tests
  • Spirometry (forced vital capacity [FVC])
  • Diffusing capacity of the lung for carbon monoxide (DLCO)
  • Lung volume measurement (total lung capacity [TLC])
  • Arterial blood gas testing
  • Questionnaire, like the University of California San Diego Shortness of Breath Questionnaire

No matter how chronic ILD with worsening fibrosis affects you, it’s important to understand what’s happening inside your body so you can take action and make informed decisions about your care.
