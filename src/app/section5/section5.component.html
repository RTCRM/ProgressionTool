Risk of Acute Exacerbations

Chronic ILD with fibrosis may worsen because of an acute exacerbation (AE). An AE is a sudden and unpredictable worsening that can result in hospitalization and can even be fatal. The symptoms of an AE are similar for people with chronic ILD with worsening fibrosis as with people who have IPF, but having an AE is more common in people with IPF. It’s also important to remember that not all types of ILD have AEs.


During an acute exacerbation, you could experience:

Breathing becoming more difficult than usual

Worsening cough

Fever or flu-like symptoms

If you experience the symptoms above, be sure to call your healthcare provider right away.


An acute exacerbation can occur at any time during the course of the disease—from diagnosis to more advanced chronic ILD with worsening fibrosis.


People who have an acute exacerbation may have a higher risk of another acute exacerbation. A person’s condition after an acute exacerbation can vary, but it often results in new lung damage that worsens lung function.
